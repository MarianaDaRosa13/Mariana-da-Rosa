About me

I am a PhD candidate in Applied Mathematics at the University of Campinas (UNICAMP), Brazil. My research focuses on the theory and numerical methods of constrained nonlinear optimization.

I am particularly interested in constraint qualifications, local error bounds, augmented Lagrangian methods, mathematical programs with complementarity constraints, and optimization problems with cardinality constraints. I also work on applications of optimization to data science and molecular distance geometry.

01

Research

Constraint qualifications

Constant-rank-type conditions, quasinormality, reducibility, local geometry of feasible sets, and local error bounds.

Augmented Lagrangian methods

Global convergence, boundedness of multipliers, lower-level constraints, and algorithms for structured nonlinear programs.

Structured optimization

Mathematical programs with complementarity constraints, cardinality constraints, exact penalties, and smoothing methods.

Applications

Variable selection in cancer omics data, molecular distance geometry, and emerging optimization models in energy pricing.
